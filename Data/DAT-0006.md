==With match tickets out of reach for most fans — group stage seats regularly exceeding $1,000 apiece — host cities across North America leaned into free and low-cost fan festivals as a way to let more people share in the World Cup.== City officials and league executives spoke with KSHB about what that access means and what they're hoping it will do for their communities. [[CED-0004]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-16-45

---

**Zohran Mamdani** (Mayor of New York City), on the decision to make all five NYC boroughs' fan zones free of charge:

=="Every fan should be able to watch the greatest tournament on earth without dipping into their savings."== [[CED-0004]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-16-57

---

**Josh Matlow** (Toronto City Councilor), after public backlash forced a reversal of a proposed $10 entry fee at the city's official fan festival:

=="Toronto residents already paid $180 million to host the games, and tickets to the stadium are cost prohibitive for most people. At the very least, an event that was promised to residents, no matter their means, so that everyone can be part of the World Cup, will be free and accessible to all."== [[CED-0004]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-17-43

---

**Camilo Durana** (Chief Business Officer, Major League Soccer), on what the tournament represents for soccer's long-term growth in North America:

"Historically, after every men's World Cup that the U.S. men's national team has participated in, we've seen about a 10% to 20% growth in interest in MLS. The idea that this World Cup, in its expanded form, played on home soil with MLS playing such an important role from a hosting perspective, we think it can have an even greater impact, and that's the opportunity for us."
