NPR's Chloe Veltman reports on three craft breweries in the three World Cup host countries — Rey Arbol Brewing Co. (Mexico), Headlands Brewing (U.S.), and Cabin Brewing Co. (Canada) — which brewed a shared World Cup-themed beer, "Common Ground." None of them is a World Cup sponsor.

---

**Alejandro Gomez** (Rey Arbol Brewing Co., Mexico): "It's a Mexican lager."

**Ryan Frank** (Headlands Brewing, U.S.): "That is like a West Coast IPA."

**Haydon Dewes** (Cabin Brewing Co., Canada): "And then us up in Canada, most of our beers are hop driven. So we thought, let's go for a dry-hopped Mexican lager."

**NPR (Chloe Veltman):** All three breweries share the same recipe, but each gives the product its own spin.

**Haydon Dewes:** "All three of us ended up designing our own label for the beer."

**Alejandro Gomez** (has created World Cup-themed beers before, but says this one is about more than just making a product): "When I go to California or Canada, they will treat me like family."

**Ryan Frank** (on naming the trinational collaboration Common Ground): "Building bridges and knowing what's important in life — and for us, that's soccer and beer."

---

**NPR (Chloe Veltman):** The official World Cup messaging is also about building bridges, but that rhetoric stands in sharp contrast to geopolitical realities — tensions between the U.S., Mexico and Canada over trade tariffs are high, and the beermakers feel the friction.

**Ryan Frank:** "There are tariffs slapped on any European-grown hops, which are really critical to some of our core brands."

**Andres Martinez** (codirector, Arizona State University's Great Game Lab, which explores the connection between sport media and geopolitics): "It's true that when it comes to the actual soccer, we've developed a very healthy, vibrant rivalry between the three countries... But we're also linked together in this very symbiotic relationship... To see craft beers across the three countries coming together like this, it's a bittersweet reminder of what we were hoping to see a lot more of, right?"

---

**NPR (Chloe Veltman):** At Headlands Brewing's North Berkeley location, fans watch Mexico play South Africa while drinking the collaboration beer.

**Roberto Mandujano** (Mexico fan, sipping a pint of Common Ground): "Three different ways — right? — three different tastebuds come together and make something cool... We live in a world where everyone wants to make everything political, right? But I think it's more of, I guess, we're all here for soccer, right?"
