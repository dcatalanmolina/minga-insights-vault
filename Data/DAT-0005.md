**Interviewer:** Andrew, the scale of this tournament is unlike anything the US has hosted before. What keeps you up at night?

**Andrew Giuliani** (Executive Director, White House FIFA World Cup 2026 Task Force): ==This entire country's police force is leaning in. It is an unbelievable problem set. We're going to do everything in our power to make sure that nothing goes wrong.== [[CDE-0003]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-00-00

**Interviewer:** One challenge is dealing with fans from dozens of different countries, each with different cultures around football. How are local police departments preparing for that?

**Andrew Giuliani:** ==What may look like a riot to a local police officer, that may actually end up being something that's just standard practice for a soccer fan from that country. We've been working with law enforcement from across the country on exactly that kind of cultural gap.== [[CDE-0003]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-00-38

**John Przepiorka** (Philadelphia Police Chief Inspector): Language is one of the biggest operational challenges. Officers are being equipped with translation technology in over 50 languages.

**Interviewer:** Stadium security is one layer, but what about the perimeter — bars, transit hubs, fan zones?

**JP Hayslip** (Philadelphia Eagles Director of Security): We don't want a person to get to our property who doesn't have a ticket. That's the baseline. Everything we do operationally works backward from that.

**Interviewer:** The FBI has raised concerns about terrorism. How seriously is law enforcement taking that threat?

**Christopher Raia** (FBI Deputy Director): ==The concern around homegrown extremists and small-scale attacks is real. Events like this create opportunities, and we're not naive about that.== [[CDE-0003]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-01-48

**John Cohen** (Center for Internet Security Executive Director): ==Secondary locations — the fan parks, the transit hubs — are the vulnerable areas. They lack the resources and the protocols that stadiums have.== [[CDE-0003]] ^id-2026-06-08--22-02-14

**Chuck Wexler** (Police Executive Research Forum Executive Director): Police departments have more resignations and retirements and less people signing up. Staffing for an event of this scale is a real constraint.

**Interviewer:** What does success look like when it's all over?

**Andrew Giuliani:** If on July 20, ESPN is talking about an amazing game for the World Cup final...then we've done our job.
