# Insight

> [!info] A statement for a foundational insight or problem to solve. 

<!-- Use job stories to type foundational insights that reflect users' or customers' stable needs, motivations, and goals. For example, "As a (role) I need a way to (achieve goal) in order to (fulfill underlying motivation)" or "When (situation), I want to (goal), so I can (expected outcome)."-->

When I can't attend World Cup matches — because tickets are out of reach or I don't feel safe gathering in official public spaces — I want to celebrate the tournament through my own neighborhood's places and rituals, so I can feel part of the event without leaving my community.

# Relevance

> [!info] Why is this insight important or relevant? 
> 

<!-- Add bullets connecting the insight to things your team or other teams care about (e.g., market trends, product roadmap, or current company strategies)-->

- WFC is not a World Cup sponsor, so it cannot compete inside official venues or FIFA fan festivals. This insight shows that a large share of fan life happens in a third space — neighborhood bars, plazas, sticker swaps, murals, local breweries — that official sponsors and host committees largely do not program.
- The Common Ground case ([[DAT-0009]]) is a working template: a non-sponsor brand earned fan attention by celebrating what fans already care about (community, cross-border identity, watching together) in local venues, without using protected tournament marks.
- Fear and affordability mean some of the most passionate fan communities are the least reachable by official channels ([[DAT-0008]]). A brand that shows up respectfully in trusted neighborhood spaces can build loyalty precisely where sponsors are absent.
- Concrete opportunity areas for WFC: sponsor neighborhood watch parties in immigrant enclaves, support existing rituals (sticker-trading meetups, local murals, food vendors), and partner with local businesses that fans already treat as their World Cup home base.
