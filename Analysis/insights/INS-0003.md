# Relevance

> [!info] Why is this insight important or relevant? 
> 

<!-- Add bullets connecting the insight to things your team or other teams care about (e.g., market trends, product roadmap, or current company strategies)-->

- WFC is not a World Cup sponsor, so it cannot compete inside official venues or FIFA fan festivals. This insight shows that a large share of fan life happens in a third space — neighborhood bars, plazas, sticker swaps, murals, local breweries — that official sponsors and host committees largely do not program. <!-- COV: GAP — WFC's non-sponsor status is not traceable to any file in the vault, and "a large share of fan life" is a quantitative claim resting only on anecdotal street scenes from one Queens report (DAT-0008). -->
- The Common Ground case ([[DAT-0009]]) is a working template: a non-sponsor brand earned fan attention by celebrating what fans already care about (community, cross-border identity, watching together) in local venues, without using protected tournament marks. <!-- COV: GAP — "without using protected tournament marks" is not verifiable from DAT-0009, which never discusses marks or rights and describes the beer as "World Cup-themed"; "working template" also generalizes from a single documented event. -->
- Fear and affordability mean some of the most passionate fan communities are the least reachable by official channels ([[DAT-0008]]). A brand that shows up respectfully in trusted neighborhood spaces can build loyalty precisely where sponsors are absent. <!-- COV: GAP — the first sentence traces to DAT-0008, but the loyalty claim is an unsupported leap: no source documents brand loyalty forming this way, and DAT-0008 says the fearful fans in question stay home rather than gather in neighborhood spaces. -->
- Concrete opportunity areas for WFC: sponsor neighborhood watch parties in immigrant enclaves, support existing rituals (sticker-trading meetups, local murals, food vendors), and partner with local businesses that fans already treat as their World Cup home base. <!-- COV: PASS — each opportunity area maps to a ritual or venue documented in DAT-0008 (sticker trading, the Messi mural, food vendors, restaurants and bars), though note the mural's scale is thinly evidenced per the Evidence-table annotation above. -->
