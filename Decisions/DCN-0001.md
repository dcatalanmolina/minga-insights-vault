# Decision

> [!info] A decision that impacts research, product, or GTM strategy, supported by insights.

<!-- Summarize a major decision that changes or refines strategy. The statement below needs to be specific enough for you to track the outcomes of the decision over time.-->

WFC will pilot sponsorship of neighborhood-level watch parties and existing fan rituals (sticker-trading meetups, mural gatherings, brewery collaborations) in three fan-dense, non-stadium neighborhoods during the group stage — instead of bidding for official fan-zone signage — to build brand affinity where official sponsors can't reach fans.

## Expected Outcomes
<!-- Add bullets with the expected short- and long-term outcomes of this decision. These will help you track whether the decision had any impact on strategy.-->

- Local media or social posts mention WFC-sponsored watch parties or rituals in the target neighborhoods during the group stage.
- Attendance or participation at sponsored events (e.g., headcount at watch parties, sticker-swap turnout) grows across the group stage rather than staying flat or declining.
- Post-tournament brand awareness or sentiment surveys in the target neighborhoods show WFC recognition higher than the pre-tournament baseline.
- At least one earned-media story frames WFC as a community-first alternative to official sponsors.

## Potential Risks
<!-- Add bullets with potential risks or negative results. These will help you monitor whether the decision needs to be reversed or changed before it's too late.-->

- Local partners (breweries, community organizations) decline association with a non-sponsor brand out of concern over trademark or rights conflicts with FIFA.
- Fans in the target neighborhoods perceive the sponsorship as opportunistic or inauthentic, generating backlash instead of affinity.
- Turnout at sponsored events is too low to justify the budget, given how informal and organic these rituals are.
